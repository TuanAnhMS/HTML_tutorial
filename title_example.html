What is the Title Tag?

The <title> tag defines the title of an HTML document. It appears in:

  • Browser tab
  • Browser history
  • Search engine results
  • Browser bookmarks
  • Social media previews

Basic Syntax

<!DOCTYPE html> <html> <head> <title>Page Title Goes Here</title> </head> <body> <!-- Page content --> </body> </html>
Tab: Example 1 - Simple Title

This is how the title appears in the browser tab

Example 1: Simple Title

<title>My Website</title>

The simplest form of a title tag.

Example 2: Descriptive Title

<title>Welcome to My Blog - Travel & Technology</title>

Include keywords and description for better SEO.

Example 3: Title with Separator

<title>Contact Us | My Company</title> <title>About - Tech Academy ¬∑ Learn Coding</title> <title>Products & Services / My Shop</title>

Use separators (|, ¬∑, /) to divide page name from site name.

Example 4: Dynamic Title

<!-- Changing title based on content --> <title>User Profile: John Doe | My App</title> <title>Product: JavaScript Guide | Amazon</title> <title>Search Results: "web design" | Google</title>

Titles can vary based on page content or user input.

Example 5: E-commerce Title

<title>Buy Premium Headphones - 50% Off | TechStore</title>

Effective for product pages with call-to-action and discount info.

Element Where It Appears Impact
<title> Browser tab, History, Bookmarks High - First thing users see
Search Results Google, Bing, etc. Critical - Affects click-through rate
Social Share Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn High - Used as link preview text
Browser History Ctrl/Cmd + H Medium - Helps users find pages

‚úì Best Practices for Titles

  • Keep it concise: 30-60 characters is ideal (50-60 chars for search engines)
  • Include keywords: Use relevant words early in the title
  • Be descriptive: Clearly describe page content
  • Unique titles: Every page should have a unique title
  • Brand name: Include your site/company name
  • Use separators: | ¬∑ - / to separate elements
  • Avoid: Don't use all caps, special characters, or misleading text
  • Front-load important words: Put most important info first
  • Update regularly: Refresh titles for seasonal or trending content
